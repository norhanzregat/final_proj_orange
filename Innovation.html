Assignment 2

Entrepreneurship



In the Entrepreneurship course, we reviewed a set of topics that aim to develop a deep understanding of the concept of entrepreneurship and the role of the entrepreneur in society. We started by reviewing the previous sessions to reinforce the concepts and confirm their understanding, then we moved on to talk about the importance of innovation and how to stimulate it as an essential part of the success of entrepreneurial projects. We also learned about the basic qualities and skills that an entrepreneur must possess, such as the ability to lead and take risks. We also discussed the challenges that entrepreneurs may face and ways to deal with them to ensure continued success. To add a practical aspect, we reviewed inspiring success stories, such as the story of Steve Jobs, who challenged traditional standards in the field of technology, and the story of Jack Ma, who was able to establish one of the largest companies in the world despite his humble beginnings. These topics combined were aimed at providing us with a comprehensive understanding of how to achieve success in the world of entrepreneurship.


Assignment 3

Design Thinking




The group was divided into smaller groups, each group studied a specific area and identified its problems in a positive and open way, using brainstorming and design thinking through three common questions, which are:
1.How might we?
2.Year Goal?
3. Can we ?
After the voting process, brainstorming and analysis, (Gaza) City was approved as the target that will now be studied during the three points and prototypes will be made for it.

Here, several problems facing the sector in Gaza City were studied, including health, educational, pollution, and many other problems. A vote was taken on the problems that it was believed would be very important to solve.

After selecting all the potential problems facing the Gaza Strip, a vote was taken on the most important problems from the point of view of the team members as a whole and from all groups. Several sections were chosen that we wanted to address, including health, energy, and food. Under each of these names, the most important problems that we will be able to solve were identified and voted on.

After completing the identification of the sections, we now began the feasibility study for each section and determining what we can do in this section. We voted on the most popular options and the options that everyone preferred and saw as appropriate for obtaining a radical solution to help the people of Gaza.

Najahna platform, which was introduced to support entrepreneurs and small projects in Jordan. This platform is part of an initiative provided by Orange Jordan and aims to empower individuals to start their own businesses by offering fully non-refundable grants to fund these projects. Najahna provides opportunities for individuals with innovative ideas or ready business plans to receive financial support and the necessary guidance to successfully launch and implement their ventures.

The platform was thoroughly explained by lawyer Mr. Omar, who detailed how to benefit from the grant, the requirements for applying, and the steps to create and run a project with full support. Najahna offers an encouraging environment for entrepreneurs in Jordan, increasing their chances of success and sustainability through financial assistance and ongoing advisory services.

We had the opportunity to attend a detailed and important session on Jordanian labor law and company formation at ODV-Irbid, led by the experienced NRC lawyers, Mr. Murad and Mr. Anas, along with the skilled trainer Balqees Alaarag. The session provided an in-depth explanation of labor laws in Jordan, giving us insight into key regulations that define the rights and responsibilities of both employees and employers. Additionally, we explored the different types of companies allowed in Jordan and the legal distinctions between each type, focusing on their respective rights and obligations.

Moreover, the session included a thorough discussion on the process of obtaining work permits for both Jordanians and Syrians, covering the requirements and procedures involved. This workshop was an invaluable opportunity for acquiring essential legal knowledge that is particularly beneficial for trainees and students at the start of their careers, as it helps them grasp the foundational aspects and challenges of the labor market in Jordan. Overall, it was an enriching learning experience, full of crucial information for anyone aiming to deepen their understanding of the Jordanian job market and the nature of employment within it.

During one of the special meetings, we attended a session with Engineer Qusay Malahameh, who shared with us his inspiring experience in entrepreneurship. Engineer Qusay started his career as an ambitious engineer, and thanks to his efforts and experience with a team of engineers, he was able to establish his first company, Menalab, which was the beginning of an ambitious idea aimed at developing the electronics and 3D tools industry. Initially, the company focused on small and simple projects, but it quickly expanded to include a wider range of technical fields. Today, Menalab has become a leading institution that provides advanced services in the field of designing and manufacturing 3D tools, developing smart electronics, in addition to providing training courses and educational workshops that aim to enable individuals and communities to benefit from these technologies to improve their daily lives. One of the company's most notable achievements is that it contributes to facilitating access to advanced tools for young developers and students, making technology accessible to everyone. The session was an inspiration for us, as we learned about the importance of persistence and hard work to turn ideas into a tangible reality that serves the world.
