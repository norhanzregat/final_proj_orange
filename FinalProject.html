The final project I am presenting is a Smart Stethoscope, a groundbreaking healthcare device designed to measure vital signs such as blood oxygen levels, blood pressure, heart rate, and body temperature. This innovative stethoscope aims to integrate multiple sensors and modern technologies to provide a compact, user-friendly, and accurate tool for medical professionals and patients alike. By incorporating sensors like the MAX30100 for heart rate and oxygen measurement, the MLX90615 for body temperature, and an OLED display, this stethoscope will serve as a reliable, all-in-one solution for monitoring critical health data in real-time.

In this documentation, I will delve into various aspects of the project, starting from the design phase of the stethoscope and moving through the 3D printing process to create its parts. The design of the sensors, their integration, and the process of programming them using Arduino will also be covered in detail. I will explain how each sensor is carefully selected, assembled, and programmed to ensure optimal performance and precision. Additionally, I will walk through the process of designing and printing the necessary components using 3D printing technology, which allows for custom, ergonomic designs tailored to the stethoscope's intended use.

Beyond the technical aspects, I will also cover the creative side of the project, including the design of the logo and branding, to ensure the device has a professional and cohesive identity. This documentation will offer insights into the entire journey of developing the Smart Stethoscope—from conception and design to the final product—highlighting both the technical expertise and creative effort involved in bringing this innovative medical tool to life.

The idea for this project stemmed from a common problem faced by medical professionals. As a doctor, the stethoscope is one of the most important and widely used tools. It’s a device doctors carry with them throughout their day, whether in the clinic or hospital. However, while the stethoscope is essential for listening to a heartbeat, it only measures one aspect of a patient’s health – heart rate. This limitation got me thinking: why should a doctor need multiple devices to measure other vital signs such as blood pressure, temperature, and oxygen levels? Why not combine all of these functions into one convenient tool that can display real-time data on a small screen, making it easier for doctors to quickly and accurately assess the health of their patients?

The initial concept for this project was inspired by artificial intelligence, which led us to envision a smart stethoscope. With the help of AI, we began to realize the possibility of integrating multiple vital-sign monitoring features into a single device. This sparked a series of ideas and innovations that we started to explore. The smart stethoscope we conceived would not only listen to heartbeats, but also measure blood pressure, oxygen saturation, and body temperature, all in one device. By merging these features into one tool, doctors can save time and effort while providing more comprehensive care to their patients. This project was born from a simple desire to address a common issue and create a more efficient and effective tool for healthcare professionals.

We began the project by researching and investigating the sensors and data sheets required to bring the smart stethoscope concept to life. This initial stage focused on understanding each component's specifications, identifying compatible sensors, and exploring ways to integrate them effectively. We sought information on each part to ensure it met our functional and technical needs, paying special attention to details that would support accurate data collection. Additionally, we researched vendors within Jordan, such as Wasleh, to source the necessary parts and reviewed the pricing options for each sensor. This comprehensive approach allowed us to develop a clear plan for assembling a simplified prototype and to gather the essential tools and materials, ensuring we were well-prepared to start building an initial model of the device.

Research and planning began to select all the components needed for the project, where it was determined that the Arduino Nano was the most suitable in terms of small size and efficiency in performing the required tasks. In addition, a breadboard was chosen to facilitate the temporary installation of wires and components before moving on to the final connections, allowing for safe and flexible testing of the system. The appropriate wires were also identified for the various connections, so that they fit the components and ensure the stability of signals between devices.

Regarding the sensors, the MAX30100 sensor was chosen to measure heart rate and blood oxygen levels, in addition to the MLX90614 sensor to accurately measure temperature. Other sensors that may be necessary for the project were also identified. After collecting all the available information and options, they were organized in an Excel file that includes accurate details for each component, such as the required quantity, prices, part number, and technical specifications for each item.

After that, a purchase order was placed for all the required components from the Microelectronics website, which provides a wide range of high-quality electronic components. The expected arrival timeline was determined, to ensure that all parts are available on time, which ensures smooth and efficient work continuity.

After placing an order for the necessary parts from Microelectronics, the required components were received on time. Once the parts arrived, we started opening the packages and organizing the components according to the pre-planned layout. All the parts were checked for integrity and number, and we made sure that each component matched the specifications listed in the Excel file. With the basic components such as the Arduino Nano, the Breadboard, the wires, and the various sensors in place, we started the initial assembly phase, where the parts were assembled on the Breadboard and prepared for initial testing. Work is now underway to conduct the necessary tests and connections to ensure that all the components are working efficiently before moving on to the next steps in the project.

After receiving the components, accurate measurements were taken for each piece required for the project, such as the measurements of the sensors, electronics, and wires, in addition to the measurements of the Arduino Nano. Precision measuring tools were used to ensure that all dimensions matched the initial design. These measurements are an essential step in the design phase, as the necessary data was collected to determine how to effectively distribute the components on the mailboard, as well as to determine the appropriate distances and paths for wires and connections between the pieces. Through this process, all components were prepared in an organized manner to ensure that the final design would be practical and consistent with the actual dimensions of the components, facilitating the implementation and connections phase later on.

After identifying and connecting the required components, we began by taking precise measurements for each part based on their real-world dimensions. These measurements served as the foundation for designing each part in Fusion 360. Working in Fusion 360 requires great accuracy when defining measurements, as the program provides tools like "Dimensions" to set distances and angles precisely, as well as "Scale" to ensure the dimensions match real-world proportions. Taking accurate measurements involves using tools such as rulers or micrometers and considering slight tolerances that might occur when translating real-world measurements into 3D models. Fusion 360 also offers "Inspect" tools to verify these measurements during the design process to ensure alignment and consistency. Once the design is complete, we convert it into a printable format, ready for 3D printing. At this stage, it’s essential to check that all parts will fit together correctly, ensuring the pieces will connect and align properly after printing. This step guarantees that the design is not only accurate but also functional in the physical assembly.

Initial designs for the speakers were first developed, as they were not yet approved, and were intended to study how the parts would be arranged inside the speaker. Although these initial designs were an important step in understanding how the components would fit together, they were not the final design. These designs were later discarded because they would have taken up too much space during the printing process, and they were too large, which would have affected the efficiency of the printing. The parts were also not well arranged in the design, as they were all spread out in a large width, which made the assembly and coordination not ideal. After evaluating these designs, we moved towards another design that was more accurate and suitable for the project. I will now explain this final design that was approved, which is based on better organization of the components to ensure optimal performance when actually printing and assembling.

The final design of the stethoscope has now been adopted vertically, with all components arranged within this model in a thoughtful and effective manner. The design is based on the principle of integrating the parts in a manner similar to a real stethoscope, ensuring that the stethoscope is of an appropriate size and easy to use. Through the vertical arrangement, it was ensured that all components, such as sensors, screen and charging, are integrated within the body in a way that ensures maintaining the small size of the stethoscope, making it easy for the doctor to carry and use while examining patients. The final design was also implemented using 3D modeling technology in Fusion 360, which allows us to accurately determine the dimensions and ensure that all the parts will fit perfectly together when assembled. After completing the 3D design, we will begin the printing process using the 3D printer, where the parts will be printed according to these precise measurements. Through this process, we expect to obtain highly accurate printed parts that can be easily assembled, ensuring that the stethoscope will be ready for practical use, while maintaining its small size and effectiveness in medical examination.

The project's sensors were programmed, and I prepared libraries for each sensor separately to ensure that they interact properly with other components. MAX30102 Library: This library is dedicated to controlling the MAX30102 sensor, which is used to measure heart rate and blood oxygen levels. The library reads data from the sensor and converts it into values that can be processed using the Arduino. This library was used to initialize the sensor and set the necessary parameters to obtain an accurate reading of heart rate and oxygen levels. MLX96014 Library: This library is responsible for dealing with the MLX96014 sensor, which measures temperature with high accuracy. The sensor was programmed to read data periodically and display the measured temperature on an OLED screen. The library provides easy interaction with the sensor and helps in reading sensory data and converting it into an understandable value. Each library was carefully configured, and the codes for each sensor were included in the main program to deal with each sensor individually smoothly and efficiently.
